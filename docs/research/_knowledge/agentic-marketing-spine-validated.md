## Claim

beacon's growth-loop spine (DEFINE → RESEARCH → GENERATE → REFINE/RANK → POST → ANALYZE
→ LEARN) is a **superset of the proven industry-standard pattern** — every surveyed OSS
scheduler (Postiz, Mixpost, Listmonk), commercial AI tool (Blotato, ContentStudio,
Jasper), and canonical n8n pipeline implements Trigger→Research→Generate→Approve→Publish→
Log→Report. Therefore the spine is low-risk to build **except** for one stage:
**autonomous "what to post/make next" planning is shipped by NO verified incumbent** —
every market "AI coach" is *reactive critique*, not predictive planning. That stage is
beacon's genuine moat **and** its least-proven claim, so it must be sequenced **last**,
after the proven `evergreen-recycle` LEARN primitive (detect high-KPI rows → requeue
winners, as Hypefury/ContentStudio ship). Two non-negotiable preconditions for any LEARN
rigor: (1) resolve KPI **per funnel layer** with layer-appropriate metrics (TOFU=reach,
MOFU=trust, BOFU=conversion), never one global engagement rate; (2) log **selection
propensity + decision context** (`post_decisions`: `(context, action, reward, p)`) from
the first POST — it is near-zero cost now and **cannot be backfilled**, so skipping it
permanently caps counterfactual/off-policy evaluation.

## Use

Recall when planning or reviewing any beacon growth-loop build sequencing. It forbids
making the roadmap depend on autonomous-planning landing, mandates per-layer KPI and
day-one propensity logging, and licenses borrowing the spine confidently from proven OSS
(read-only for AGPL/GPL refs; vendor only MIT/BSD/Apache).
